# 📊 Sales Profitability Business Case Study

A real-world business analytics case study focused on evaluating product profitability, sales channel performance, discounting strategies, and SKU rationalization using Microsoft Excel.

---

## 💼 Business Problem

Businesses often struggle to identify which products, customers, and sales channels generate sustainable profits. Inconsistent discounting, low-margin products, and inefficient sales strategies can reduce overall profitability despite strong sales performance.

This case study analyzes historical sales data to identify profitable products, evaluate channel performance, assess discounting practices, and recommend actions to improve business profitability.

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes historical sales transactions to evaluate business profitability across products, customers, and sales channels.

Using Microsoft Excel, the analysis focuses on gross margin performance, SKU profitability, discounting behavior, and customer contribution. The objective is to transform raw sales data into actionable business insights that support strategic decision-making.

## 🎯 Business Objectives

The primary objectives of this analysis are:

- Evaluate product-level profitability using Gross Margin and Gross Margin %.
- Compare profitability across different sales channels.
- Identify high-performing and low-performing SKUs.
- Analyze the impact of discounting on business profitability.
- Detect loss-making products and customers contributing to revenue.
- Provide business recommendations to improve profitability and pricing decisions.

## 📈 Analysis

The analysis was divided into three major business areas:

### 1. Product & Channel Profitability

Evaluated product profitability using Gross Margin and Gross Margin %. Compared business performance across different sales channels to identify the most and least profitable products and channels.

---

### 2. Discounting Impact

Reviewed historical discounting practices to understand pricing inconsistencies and their impact on revenue and profitability. Developed a structured methodology for future discount decisions based on historical sales patterns.

---

### 3. SKU Rationalization

Identified products generating negative margins despite contributing to revenue. Evaluated customer profitability and analyzed which sales channels carried the highest number of loss-making SKUs.

## 💡 Key Insights

The analysis provided insights into:

- High-performing and low-performing products.
- Profitability differences across sales channels.
- Customers contributing high revenue with poor profitability.
- Pricing inconsistencies caused by discounting.
- Products requiring rationalization due to negative margins.

## 📌 Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations can improve business performance:

- Review pricing strategy for low-margin products.
- Standardize discount policies across customers and sales channels.
- Focus sales efforts on highly profitable products.
- Regularly monitor customer profitability instead of sales value alone.
- Review loss-making SKUs before continuing future sales.
- Build periodic profitability dashboards for management reporting.
